# Get TurboSites.io Found on Google (RGV Local SEO)

The site's on-page SEO is done (metadata, sitemap, robots, structured data, local
keywords). That gets you *indexable*. This checklist is what actually gets you
*found* by people searching in the Rio Grande Valley. Work top to bottom, the top
items matter most.

---

## Do today (biggest wins)

### 1. Google Business Profile (this is #1 for local)
This is what puts you in the Google Map pack and "web design near me" results.
- Go to https://business.google.com and create a profile for **TurboSites.io**.
- Business type: **Service-area business** (you go to clients, no storefront).
  Hide your address, and add service areas: **McAllen, Mission, Edinburg, Pharr,
  Brownsville, Harlingen, Weslaco**.
- Primary category: **Website Designer**. Add secondary: **Marketing Agency**,
  **Internet Marketing Service**.
- Fill in everything: phone, website (https://turbosites.io), hours, services
  (list your 3 plans), and a short description with "Rio Grande Valley."
- Add **at least 5 photos** (your logo, past work screenshots, a headshot).
- **Verify** the profile (Google mails a code or verifies by phone/video). Nothing
  ranks until you verify, so do this first.

### 2. Google Search Console (tell Google to crawl you)
- Go to https://search.google.com/search-console and add **turbosites.io**.
- Verify (easiest: it may auto-verify if the domain is on Vercel, or add the DNS
  TXT record they give you).
- Submit your sitemap: enter `sitemap.xml` under **Sitemaps**.
- Use **URL Inspection** on `https://turbosites.io` → **Request indexing**. Repeat
  for `/compare` and `/projects`.

## Do this week

### 3. Get your first 5 Google reviews
Reviews are the single biggest lever for local ranking after GBP itself.
- Ask every happy client (past el-hilo, B2Z Builders, anyone) to leave a Google
  review. Even 5 reviews moves you up fast.
- Grab your review link from the Business Profile ("Get more reviews") and text it
  to them. Make it one tap.

### 4. Lock down NAP consistency
Google trusts you more when your **N**ame, **A**ddress/area, **P**hone match
everywhere. Use the exact same business name and phone on:
- The website, Google Business Profile, Facebook, Instagram, and any directory.

### 5. List in a few local directories
Free citations that build local trust:
- Yelp, Nextdoor, Bing Places (https://www.bingplaces.com), Apple Business Connect.
- Your local chamber of commerce (McAllen, Mission) if you join.

## Ongoing (keeps you climbing)

### 6. Post to your Business Profile weekly
GBP has a "posts" feature. A short weekly post (a new project, an offer, a tip)
signals you're active and nudges ranking. Takes 2 minutes.

### 7. Add a page or section per city (later)
When you have time, a short "Web Design in McAllen," "Web Design in Edinburg," etc.
page helps you rank for each city individually. Ask me and I'll build these.

### 8. Add GA4 so you can see what's working
Google Analytics 4 shows where visitors come from. Ask me to wire it up when ready
(it also pairs with the "Advanced GA4 analytics" you sell in the Pro plan).

## Honest timeline
- **Indexed in Google:** a few days after Search Console.
- **Showing in the local map pack:** often 1 to 3 weeks after a verified Business
  Profile with a few reviews.
- **Ranking for competitive terms:** 1 to 3+ months.

The Business Profile + reviews are what move fastest, so if you only do two things
this week, do #1 and #3.
